DOOR DESIGN FOR MOUSE HOME CAGE EXPERIMENT

Jan - Apr 2022

During my four month co-op term at the Djavad Mowafaghian Centre for Brain Health with the Murphy lab, one of my tasks was to design the components needed for a door tunnel for mouse home cage experiments. My design consists of three main components:

  1. Thin Door
  2. Door Tunnel
  3. Mount for the Servomotor

I designed the door tunnel to be long enough to enable a full 180-degree swing for the door. The two holes in the front side of the tunnel are meant to be used for screwing it to the original tunnel. The door was designed to be smaller than the extruded cut so that it could move freely (mice could easily push through it) and fall by gravity. A thin metal cylindrical rod was used to attach the door to the tunnel to enable smooth rotation.

The door tunnel is designed with a small slot on the side to use a latch for holding the door in place. The shape of the slot reflects that of the latch which goes through the hole. The latch is controlled by a servomotor sitting on a servo mount screwed to the side of the door tunnel; the latch has been designed with a separate slot to be screwed into one of the holes of the servo arm which would swing the latch through the slot to keep the door in place. The slot of the latch is bigger than the screw in order to enable travel of the pin/screw in the slot.
